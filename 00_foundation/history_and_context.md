# History And Context

Lace developed across many regions and methods. This repo is not trying to be a museum catalog, but historical context matters because different lace families look different for structural reasons.

## Important broad families

- **Needle lace**: built with needle and thread, often free-form over a pattern.
- **Bobbin lace**: made by twisting and crossing many bobbin-held threads.
- **Crochet lace**: made with hook-worked loops, chains, shells, fans, pineapples, and mesh.
- **Knitted lace**: created with yarn overs, decreases, and shaped holes.
- **Tatting**: knotted rings and chains with picots.
- **Filet lace**: square mesh plus filled/open cells.
- **Cutwork**: fabric is cut away and edges are stitched.
- **Drawn-thread work**: threads removed from fabric and remaining threads bundled.
- **Machine lace**: industrial systems such as Leavers, Raschel, Schiffli, and chemical lace.
