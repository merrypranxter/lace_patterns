# Core Visual Principles

## 1. Thread and void are equal

The pattern is made by both the material and the holes.

## 2. Lace needs hierarchy

Strong lace uses multiple scales: fine mesh, medium bridges, larger motifs, border rhythm, and focal medallions.

## 3. Edges matter

Scalloped, picoted, fringed, or corded edges often define the identity of lace.

## 4. Lace is not flat print

Real lace casts shadows, has thickness, and interacts with skin, fabric, and light.

## 5. Construction method changes the look

Do not call every openwork textile "crochet lace." Method determines visual grammar.
