# Definition

Lace is an openwork textile system where pattern is created by the organized relationship between thread and void.

The important thing is not only the visible thread. Lace is made from:

- thread paths
- holes
- anchors
- crossings
- loops
- knots
- braids
- bars
- mesh grounds
- motifs
- scalloped borders
- density changes
- repeated units

A lace pattern may be made by hand or machine. It may be bobbin lace, needle lace, crochet lace, knitted lace, tatting, filet, cutwork, drawn-thread work, embroidered net, chemical lace, or industrial Raschel/Leavers lace.

## Core principle

Lace is **designed absence**.

The voids are not empty background. They are active shapes. They control rhythm, delicacy, transparency, shadow, and silhouette.
